About

I'm an Instructor of Writing & Rhetoric at the University of Central Florida, where I teach composition, research writing, and rhetoric in pop culture. My work sits at the intersection of generative AI pedagogy, postcolonial game studies, and disability-informed writing instruction.

Currently finishing my PhD in Texts & Technology (UCF, forthcoming 2027), I hold master's degrees in Rhetoric & Writing and Creative Writing from Eastern Michigan University. My scholarship has been recognized with the Kenneth Bruffee Award Honorable Mention (WPA, 2025) and the Excellence of Equity in Teaching Award (UCF, 2022–23).

I serve on the UCF Faculty Senate, chair the ePortfolio Assessment Committee, and convene the AI Across CAH group — because thinking carefully about technology in the classroom is not optional.

Teaching

I design courses that ask students to engage with writing as a civic and critical practice — whether they're analyzing a Pokémon game or drafting a public policy brief with AI assistance.

ENC 1101 ✦ CDL Quality

Composition I: Sociocultural Constructs for Community-Situated Writing

Introduces college writing through the lens of community, culture, and identity. Taught online, mixed-mode, and face-to-face at UCF.

ENC 1102 ✦ CDL Quality

Composition II: Sociocultural Constructs for Research Writing

Research writing with a social justice and community focus. Includes ePortfolio assessment. UCF Center for Distributed Learning Quality Designation.

Rhetoric & Pop Culture

Rhetoric in Pop Culture: Anime

Mixed-mode course examining how anime constructs arguments about identity, nation, and power. Students apply rhetorical frameworks to texts from Spirited Away to Attack on Titan.

Valencia College

New Student Experience & Advanced Composition

First-year success seminar, advanced composition, and creative writing at Valencia College. Built a sense of belonging for students navigating the transition to higher ed.

ESL / Professional

Corporate ESL & Business English

Beginner-through-advanced ESL instruction and business communication for professional contexts. Brings a language-justice perspective to workplace writing.
